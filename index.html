Methodology & Sources

What's under the map.

Supporting evidence-based monitoring of agricultural land conversion in Bandaragama Divisional Secretariat Division.

Study area

Bandaragama Divisional Secretariat Division (DSD), Kalutara District, Western Province, Sri Lanka. The study area is represented using a 100 m × 100 m analysis grid consisting of 14,293 cells, providing detailed spatial monitoring of agricultural land and urban conversion pressure.

Data sources

  • ESA WorldCover 2021 (land cover classification)
  • OpenStreetMap road network
  • GN Division and study area boundaries
  • Google Earth Engine (satellite processing)
  • QGIS (spatial analysis and indicator development)

Methodology

Urban conversion pressure was analysed using a 100 m analysis grid. Satellite-derived land cover, urban growth, road accessibility, and agricultural vulnerability were integrated through a weighted overlay to calculate the Urban Pressure Index (UPI) for each grid cell.

Urban Pressure Index

A weighted spatial indicator combining built-up land percentage, recent urban growth, road accessibility, and agricultural vulnerability. Each grid cell is classified into Low, Moderate, or High urban pressure to support spatial monitoring.

Conversion risk

Identifies agricultural grid cells showing increasing exposure to urban expansion by combining agricultural land extent with surrounding urban influence. The indicator highlights locations that may need closer monitoring for possible land conversion.

Citizen reports

Residents can anonymously report observed land-use changes, including location, observation type, and an optional photo. Community observations complement satellite-derived analysis and support field verification between data updates.

Data currency

Satellite-derived indicators reflect the most recent processed imagery available at the time of analysis, last updated —. Citizen reports provide additional, more immediate observations between satellite updates to support continuous monitoring.
