This web map visualises urban conversion pressure on agricultural land using spatial analysis, and lets residents report land use changes they observe directly. It's built to support evidence collection through interactive mapping and public participation — not to prescribe planning decisions.

Study area

Bandaragama DSD, Kalutara District, Western Province — a 100 m analysis grid across 14,293 cells.

Data sources

Satellite land cover classification, OpenStreetMap roads, GN Division boundaries, Google Earth Engine composites.

Analysis method

A composite Urban Pressure Index from built-up growth, urban cover, and road proximity, scored per cell in QGIS.

Methodology & Sources

What's under the map.

Study area

Bandaragama Divisional Secretariat Division, Kalutara District, Western Province, Sri Lanka. The area is discretised into a 100 m analysis grid, 14,293 cells in total.

Data sources

Satellite-derived land cover classification and composites processed in Google Earth Engine; road network and GN Division boundaries from OpenStreetMap and administrative boundary datasets; all scoring and grid analysis performed in QGIS.

Urban Pressure Index

A composite of built-up growth rate, current urban land-cover share, and proximity to the road network, computed per cell and classified into Low / Moderate / High pressure.

Conversion risk

A companion classification highlighting cells where agricultural land shows the clearest early signals of imminent conversion, independent of overall pressure class.

Citizen reports

Ground-truth observations submitted through the Report page supplement the modelled layers and help planners prioritise field verification.
